Guido's Quadratic Calculator


There once was a farmer named Guido. However, he was far from your typical happy-go-lucky hay balen', cow manure shovellen', potato growin' farmer. Guido had a strong interest in exotic animals and refrained from keeping traditional livestock. On Guido’s farm, there was a monkey named Theodore and a camel named Torstan. Guido had to keep Theodore and Torstan separated because Theodore would often climb onto Torstan’s back and this would give Torstan great anxiety. So Guido designed an enclosure that would keep Torstan and Theodore separated as well as preventing them from wandering off into the wilderness.



Guido might be good at designing an enclosure for his animals, but he is far from a mathematician and cannot figure out the optimal x & y dimensions that would give Torstan and Theodore the maximum area to flonder around. Fortunately, I have built a quadratic calculator that allows Guido to input the amount of fence that he has and returns the optimal x & y dimensions along with the maximum area of the enclosure. I have also added an input field that modifies the number of x-values, in case if Guido decides to add more enclosure areas.

    Fence Calculator Parameters:
  • values can't be negative
  • fence quantity may be a decimal
  • enclosure areas must be a whole number greater than zero
  • fence quantity may be any percieved unit of measurement

Guido is satisfied with his enclosure since he can be certain that he has granted Torstan and Theodore the maximum area to roam around. However, Guido soon came to find that it is very expensive to keep a monkey given the cost of bananas these days. Guido decided that the best way to fund his exotic animals venture would be to convert his farm into a zoo. At first things were going well and Guido was making a considerable amount of profit, however he knew that he could further optimize his profits by adjusting the admission price. Unfortunately, Guido is only minutely savvy in the business world and is no accountant, so he can't figure out how to maximize his revenue. Thankfully, I happen to be knowledgeable in both the fields of quadratics & programming and have built a revenue calculator for Guido.



Guido's revenue is represented as price per admission times number of admissions. Guido's max revenue is represented as a quadratic function. I have simplified the method of "completing the square" into a series of algebraic equations so that anyone can solve for Guido's max revenue without any knowledge on how quadratics work. To calculate Guido's max revenue, enter Guido's current price per admission and current number of admissions along with the projected market values. The projected market values are represented as a ratio of variance in price per admission to variance in number of admissions. For example, the default projected market values represent that for every $2 increase in price, 4 less people visit Guido’s zoo. This is also the equivalent of "for every $2 decrease in price, 4 more people visit Guido's zoo".

    Revenue Calculator Parameters:
  • first two values must be positive
  • prices, if fractional, must be rounded to the nearest hundredth
  • admission quantities must be whole numbers
  • only the first value may equal 0
  • one of the last two values must be negative
  • one of the last two values must be positive
  • values can't be so unproportionate that they result in a price per admission of less than $ 0.01
